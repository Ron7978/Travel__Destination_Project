Destination Overview


Welcome Audio

Paradise Island is an island in the Bahamas formerly known as Hog Island. The island, with an area of 277 hectares (685 acres) (2.8 km2/1.1 sq mi), is located just off the shore of the city of Nassau, which is itself located on the northern edge of the island of New Providence.

It is best known for the sprawling resort Atlantis with its extensive water park rides, pools, beach, restaurants, walk-in aquarium and casinos. Paradise Island is connected to the island of New Providence by two bridges that cross Nassau Harbour. The first was built in 1966 by Resorts International, and the second in the late 1990s.

Paradise Island is home to the One&Only Ocean Club, a luxury resort owned and operated by the One&Only Resorts brand within the Kerzner International. The Atlantis Paradise Island resort, which also includes the Marina Village shopping and dining area, opened in 1998.

The Coral Towers were built atop two parking garages. The Beach Tower is the original hotel building built on the site of the Paradise Beach Hotel which was destroyed by a hurricane in 1991.

Activities in Paradise Island

  • Snorkeling in the vibrant coral reefs
  • Relaxing on pristine white-sand beaches
  • Exploring the island's tropical rain-forests
  • Participating in water sports such as kayaking and paddle boarding

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit
Year-round, but peak season is during the dry months from June to September.
Consider visiting during weekdays for fewer crowds.

Currency
Local currency is Paradise Dollars (PD).
Major credit cards are widely accepted.

Language
English and Paradisean Creole are widely spoken.
Learn a few local phrases to enhance your interaction with locals.

Local Cuisine
Indulge in the rich flavors of Paradise Island's local cuisine, featuring a fusion of seafood, tropical fruits, and aromatic spices.
Try the traditional dish called "Island Delight" for a true taste of local flavors.

Weather Conditions
While the weather is generally sunny, occasional tropical showers can occur. It's advisable to carry a light raincoat or umbrella.
Check the weather forecast before planning outdoor activities.
